Hello, everyone.

I live in the city of Santa Cruz, Bolivia. Here in this city many people hate public transport: No online information on bus routes, (not yet arrived Street Views and other features of Google), the bus stops are not used properly, the main urban centers are congested and bus frequency is variable.
This is not good for the people and for tourists.
What I try to do, initially, is to map, manage and centralize all bus routes in the city, and then to provide users with the information they need to take buses to get from one point to another. These points can be landmarks (squares, museums, parks, fairs, supermarkets, banks, hospitals, etc ...) or places defined by users.

I think this is useful for tourists, and people like me who do not know all bus lines or has a bad memory. (do'h)

The next steps of the project are:

  • Get real-time information on the location of the buses and the approximate time it will take to get to our location. So users can find the time they need to wait for the buses.
  • Use this information to gather statistics that could be used to help the council to better organize traffic.
